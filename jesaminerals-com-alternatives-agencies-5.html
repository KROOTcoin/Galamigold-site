Goldady

At a Glance

Goldady’s marketing states it offers fractional, digitally traded ownership of physically vaulted 24-karat bars managed by EgyCash and licensed and regulated by Egyptian authorities. That positioning gives local investors online access to insured, vaulted metal without handling physical bars.

Core Features

Real-time gold pricing and an intuitive mobile app let you buy and sell in small increments. The platform offers fractional ownership backed by physical inventory, plus options to request physical bar delivery or long term vault storage. A referral program pays rewards for new signups.

Key Differentiator

The plan to pair digital trading with Egyptian vault custody is the distinct angle here. Goldady claims its custody is insured and audited and pairs local refiner backing with Egyptian vaulting. For Egypt focused traders who need local legal comfort, that local custody claim matters.

Pros

  • Physically backed holdings: Goldady emphasizes that each digital gram maps to insured, vaulted metal in Egypt which reduces counterparty exposure compared with ledger-only gold products.

  • Transparent fees and live pricing make trade execution straightforward for intraday or frequent small trades. You can see the spread and fees before you transact.

  • Multiple exit paths exist. Traders can sell back digitally, request insured delivery of bars, or opt for continued vaulting depending on liquidity needs and security preferences.

  • User experience is aimed at retail investors. Account setup, bank transfers in EGP, and simple portfolio statements reduce friction for local users.

Cons

  • Limited to Egypt. If you trade from another jurisdiction or want international vaulting, Goldady does not meet that need.

  • Physical delivery logistics can take several business days which hurts traders who need instant possession for arbitrage or private sale.

  • High volume institutional flows may hit platform limits or pricing tiers that are not competitive with OTC desks for large bars.

When It May Not Fit

If you require multi-country custody or settlement in USD, this platform is the wrong choice. If your strategy depends on immediate physical pickup or sub kilogram wholesale bars at tight spreads, you will find the delivery timing and retail-focused pricing constraining.

Who It’s For

Privately owned Egyptian individuals who prefer online banking and want insured, physically-backed gold exposure without storing metal themselves. It suits traders who value local regulation and want the option to convert to physical bars occasionally.

Real World Use Case

A Cairo-based professional tops up by bank transfer, buys fractional grams as a dollar hedge, and monitors positions via the app. When gold rallies, they sell portions back to the marketplace to cover short-term expenses and request a small bar delivery for a family gift.

Pricing

The vendor advertises transaction fees that scale with trading volume and vaulting fees of approximately 0.3% annually of the gold value billed monthly. Physical bars are priced by weight at spot plus delivery and handling.

Website: https://goldady.com

own-gold.com

At a Glance

CGT exemption for UK residents is a primary selling point because many products qualify as legal tender, which can make vault-held purchases tax advantaged for eligible UK buyers. The site pairs that tax angle with vault storage and a broad collectible catalog.

Core Features

  • Vault storage options for insured, segregated holding and optional third-party custody.
  • CGT exemption for qualifying UK residents when items meet legal tender rules.
  • A wide selection of bullion and graded coins including Britannia, American Eagle, Krugerrand, and Canadian Maple Leaf.
  • Secure payment choices such as PayPal and bank transfer plus regular price updates and market commentary.

Key Differentiator

own-gold.com combines a curated inventory of calibrated bullion and collectible coins with on-site options for secure storage and tax-aware handling. This focus makes it more retail and collector oriented than Galamigold, which targets disciplined trading and institutional capital deployment.

Pros

  • High trust signals for buyers who want physical custody options because the site lists vaulted storage and insured logistics as part of the purchase flow.
  • High-purity gold selection with detailed product descriptions helps you verify weight, purity, and provenance before buying.
  • Educational hub guides explain legal tender status and storage choices, which lowers friction for newcomers to physical precious metals.
  • International shipping capabilities let non-UK clients buy and receive insured shipments, with clear options for delivery or vaulting.
  • The combination of buy-back services and well known coin brands gives collectors fast access to graded pieces and liquidity when needed.

Cons

  • Prices track the market and will move on the spot rate, so short-term pricing stability is limited for buyers seeking fixed entry points.
  • The platform has limited digital portfolio tools; there is no built-in investment tracking or consolidated reporting for multi-item holdings.
  • High-demand items sell out; availability of specific graded coins can be constrained and restock timing is unpredictable.
  • Collectible coins often carry a premium above spot that may reduce immediate resale margins for strict spot-price investors.

When It May Not Fit

If you are outside the UK and do not benefit from CGT exemption, the main tax advantage disappears and fees for shipping plus insurance may erode the economics. If you need real-time portfolio dashboards or programmatic APIs, own-gold.com lacks those digital trading tools.

Who It’s For

Private investors in the UK who prioritize legal tender tax treatment and secure vaulting, plus collectors seeking graded, limited edition coins. Small institutional buyers looking for insured physical storage and clear provenance will also find the offering relevant.

Real World Use Case

A UK saver buys a 1 oz Britannia coin online, chooses insured vault storage, and registers the holding to qualify for CGT exemption. They receive purchase confirmation, subscribe to price alerts, and keep the asset in storage while monitoring market moves via the newsletter.

Pricing

Most coins and bars range roughly from £584 to £2,359 depending on weight and collectible premium. Free shipping applies for UK buyers and international insured shipping is available. Final prices move with the gold spot rate and can include premiums for graded items.

Website: https://own-gold.com
