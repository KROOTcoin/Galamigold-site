Bullion Capital

At a Glance

MetalDesk offers live pricing and instant execution for allocated physical bullion stored in insured vaults across multiple financial hubs. The platform supports real ownership of segregated gold, silver, and platinum with global delivery options. Investors access trading, account management, and physical delivery choices through a single marketplace interface.

Core Features

The platform combines a global trading marketplace with secure vaulting and logistics, and it supports allocated storage that keeps each investor’s holdings segregated. MetalDesk shows real time prices and lets you execute trades instantly while choosing storage or physical delivery. The service pairs trading tools for retail investors, institutional traders, and SMSFs with insured custody by established vault operators.

Key Differentiator

Bullion Capital positions itself as a regulated Australian marketplace that emphasizes actual ownership of metal rather than paper exposures. The platform links live trading to physically allocated stock in vaults, which removes counterparty custody layered risks. That ownership model and international vault footprint set it apart from custody wrappers and many exchange traded products.

Pros

Regulation and external audits back the operation, which will matter to investors requiring a transparent counterparty and formal oversight. The custody model provides full ownership and segregation of assets, and insurance coverage and global logistics let you choose where to hold or take delivery. The platform targets both individual investors and institutional flows, and the company reports industry awards for its technology and client service.

Cons

  • Limited to physical precious metals only. It does not support paper derivatives or futures strategies, so portfolio managers seeking those tools will need another venue.

  • Costs can be higher than unregulated local dealers. Trading spreads, vaulting fees, and delivery charges apply and may erode near term returns for small trades.

  • Requires familiarity with international bullion logistics. Investors unfamiliar with allocated storage, import rules, or delivery procedures will face a learning curve.

When It May Not Fit

If your mandate requires unallocated or pooled metal positions the platform is not the right choice. Clients operating from unsupported jurisdictions or without the ability to handle cross border logistics should avoid this option. Traders that need derivatives, futures access, or local dealer convenience will find gaps in the offering.

Who It’s For

This marketplace fits serious investors and institutions that want direct, segregated ownership of physical gold and silver. It also suits SMSFs that need custody solutions tied to a regulated firm and traders executing global arbitrage between vault hubs. You should be comfortable managing storage and delivery choices across jurisdictions.

Real World Use Case

A retail investor opens an account, funds it, and buys allocated gold stored in Zurich. They watch live prices on MetalDesk, execute a sale when the market moves, and either leave the metal in insured storage or request physical delivery. The process keeps ownership clear because holdings remain segregated in a named vault allocation.

Pricing

Pricing varies by metal and vault location. Trades settle with market spreads, and separate fees apply for vaulting and physical delivery. Institutional clients can negotiate subscription or volume discounts.

Website: https://bullioncapital.com

Sharps Pixley

At a Glance

Founded in 1796, Sharps Pixley traces its business back more than two centuries in London’s bullion trade. The firm pairs an online shop with a West End showroom and London vaulting options for private and institutional clients. Buyers can purchase LBMA approved coins and arrange insured storage through in-house services.

Core Features

A wide catalog includes LBMA-approved gold, silver, platinum, and palladium coins and bars sourced from recognized mints. The company provides secure vaulting and safe deposit boxes in London along with bespoke investment proposals and portfolio support. Competitive buyback offers, live market pricing, and cryptocurrency acceptance add transactional flexibility across online and in-person channels.

Key Differentiator

Sharps Pixley stands out for its centuries-old London presence combined with onsite custody and tailored client service. That mix gives buyers direct access to physical custody, face to face advisory, and provenance verification. The firm is an authorized distributor of Royal Mint products, which helps collectors and investors who value traceable origin.

Pros

A long operational history and established partnerships lend credibility when arranging significant bullion purchases. High security and insurance for stored assets reduce custody risk for clients who select London vaulting. Personalized client service and transparent market commentary help you choose specific coins or bars and understand timing and buyback mechanics.

Cons

  • Premium pricing because of the boutique, high service model increases cost compared with discount dealers.
  • Physical presence is mainly in London, so international clients face extra delivery and vaulting logistics.
  • A primary focus on UK and European markets limits direct local support outside those regions.
  • Higher minimum purchase thresholds apply for some bullion items, raising entry costs for smaller buyers.

When It May Not Fit

If you require local custody outside the United Kingdom, relying on London vaulting will add complexity and cost. If your priority is the lowest wholesale price for very large lots, this dealer’s premium model may not match market players focused on volume. If you need multiple storefronts in different countries, a dealer with a broader physical footprint will serve you better.

Who It’s For

Private investors, family offices, and institutional clients seeking secure, London-based access to physical precious metals will benefit most. The service suits buyers who value provenance, face to face advisory, and insured custody rather than the lowest possible spot markup. It also fits collectors seeking branded mint products.

Real World Use Case

A private investor visits the flagship store to buy gold for portfolio diversification. They consult a specialist, select specific coins and bars, and arrange secure vaulting with insurance. The investor then uses the online shop for follow-up purchases and relies on transparent pricing and buyback terms when adjusting holdings.

Pricing

Prices follow live bullion market rates so spreads vary by product and quantity. Sharps Pixley offers free UK next day insured delivery and publishes competitive buyback terms. Discounts apply for bulk purchases and multibuy orders depending on product and order size.

Website: https://sharpspixley.com

Dillon Gage

At a Glance

Dillon Gage holds authorization to buy directly from the U.S. Mint and the Royal Canadian Mint, giving institutional buyers direct access to mint-issued inventory. The company combines that access with a digital trading experience built around its proprietary FizTrade platform. That pairing shortens settlement steps for wholesale counterparties and dealers.

Core Features

Dillon Gage operates a real-time online marketplace through FizTrade that supports buy and sell orders for major bullion products. The firm pairs trading with secure, insured depository services in North America and full-service refining for gold, silver, platinum, palladium, and rhodium. Worldwide shipping and formal compliance and responsible sourcing policies complete the operational stack for institutional flows.

Key Differentiator

The one distinguishing capability is the combination of direct mint purchasing authority and a proprietary trading engine. That mix gives dealers and financial institutions authenticated supply and fast execution on a single platform. The setup reduces reliance on third-party sourcing for large bullion lots.

Pros

Decades of industry experience translate to established counterparty relationships and operational know-how. Global inventory is managed across multiple jurisdictions, which helps institutions access product variety and cross-border settlement options. Integrated services group trading, refining, storage, and logistics under one vendor to reduce coordination work for procurement teams. Advanced digital matching on FizTrade speeds order execution relative to manual quote chains. Authorization to purchase from major mints supports provenance and reduces authentication friction for large purchases.

Cons

  • Primarily serves wholesale and institutional clients. Small or casual retail buyers will find the platform less accessible.
  • Pricing follows spot plus premiums. Those premiums can be higher than typical retail markups for single-piece purchases.
  • Minimum transaction sizes typically suit professional and bulk buyers. Smaller investors face higher entry thresholds.
  • The brand emphasizes B2B flows and lacks consumer-facing retail marketing or storefronts.

When It May Not Fit

If your portfolio strategy targets sub-$10,000 allocations, Dillon Gage will likely be a poor fit. The platform suits buyers who need large lots and formal custody arrangements. If you prefer a retail counter with single-piece price parity and consumer marketing, look elsewhere.

Who It’s For

This marketplace fits professional traders, institutional investors, bullion dealers, jewelry manufacturers, and high-net-worth private clients. Use it when you require authenticated mint product, refined metal services, and insured storage. Choose it when you need consolidated handling of large consignments and regulated settlement.

Real World Use Case

A bank opens a business account with Dillon Gage and uses FizTrade for intraday purchases of gold and silver bullion. The bank moves purchased metal into Dillon Gage insured depositories and sends occasional loads for refining. The workflow reduces counterparty steps and centralizes custody and settlement.

Pricing

Prices change with global precious metals markets and include spot plus variable premiums. Dillon Gage reports a minimum order value of $10,000. Smaller orders may incur a $25 shipping fee and the company states no account setup charge.

Website: https://dillongage.com
